# Pitch Deck Audiences

## Branding

- **Product:** Saxe (SAXE) — Skill & AI eXecution Engine
- **Company:** data2impact
- **Reference:** "proprietary, battle-tested full-stack framework"

## Three Decks Needed

### 1. Partner Deck (MOST URGENT)

**Audience:** Consulting firms (McKinsey alumni, Bain alumni, mid-tier consultancies)
**Ask:** Try the prototype, provide feedback, sell to their clients
**Their question:** "Can this help me deliver more value to my clients, faster?"
**Key messages:**

- You already build custom tools and deliverables for client projects (manually, expensively)
- Saxe lets your consultants build enterprise tools in hours, not months — and the same platform can power your own consulting delivery (research → recommendation → presentation pipelines)
- Your clients are asking "What's our AI strategy?" — Saxe is the answer you bring them
- Early partner advantage: shape the product, preferred pricing, co-sell
  **Proof points:** data2impact built enterprise apps for McKinsey, pharma, banking, biotech. 10 production apps. Pipeline model validated end-to-end.
  **Tone:** Peer-to-peer, "we speak your language"

### 2. Enterprise Deck

**Audience:** Business managers at mid-size companies (50–1,000 employees), with backup slides for CIO/IT
**Ask:** Pilot program, initial deployment
**Their question:** "How do I actually leverage AI for my team?"
**Key messages:**

- Your team is sitting on valuable processes trapped in Excel, PowerPoint, and people's heads
- Saxe turns those into real apps — and the same platform handles consulting deliverables, offers, and other structured workflows
- IT stays in control (auth, deployment, design, governance) — approves the platform once, business units use it for any skill pipeline
- No lock-in — apps are real code, all artifacts are files in git
- Open sessions: your people can also use AI for freeform work in the same governed environment
  **Proof points:** Enterprise references (pharma, banking, biotech), compliance-ready
  **Tone:** Business-first, no jargon, "detour slides" for CIO with technical depth
  **Timing:** May need to wait for more validation / working demo

### 3. Investor Deck

**Audience:** Seed / Series A investors
**Ask:** Funding
**Their question:** "Is this a big market with a defensible product and a team that can execute?"
**Key messages:**

- $5-10B wedge market (software), $200B+ vision (enterprise AI process platform)
- Unique IP: generalized skill execution model + proprietary generators + configurable skill templates
- Not an app builder — an enterprise AI process platform where app building is the first vertical
- Team: senior A-team, founders from McKinsey/Bain, 10 production apps shipped
- Enterprise validation: pharma, banking, biotech references
- Platform play: same infrastructure serves expanding verticals — software, consulting, sales, due diligence
  **Proof points:** Production framework, enterprise clients, working skill pipeline, generalized model already architected
  **Tone:** Ambitious but grounded, numbers-driven
