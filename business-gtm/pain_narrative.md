## The 2026 Problem (Primary Hook)

> "Everyone from my CEO to my board is asking 'What's our AI strategy?' — and all I have is ChatGPT and Copilot."

The pressure to deliver AI impact is universal — from CEO down to line manager. But the tools available are either:

- **Toys** — ChatGPT, generic chat assistants (interesting but no business impact)
- **Generic corporate AI** — Microsoft Copilot, Google Gemini (convenient but shallow)
- **Developer tools** — Cursor, Windsurf, bolt.new (powerful but require technical skills)

Nobody has an answer for: **"How does a business user leverage AI to create real, lasting business value?"**

## The Background Problem (Shadow IT)

> "The tool doesn't get built. It's too expensive, too slow, too hard to fight for IT resources. So a smart employee builds it in Excel. It works. For a while. Then that person leaves, nobody understands it, it can't scale, and the business is stuck with a critical process running in a spreadsheet that nobody can maintain."

### Pain Points (from 20 years of firsthand experience)

1. **IT backlog**: Custom internal tools never get built — too expensive, too slow, can't compete for IT resources
2. **Shadow IT proliferation**: Business users build in Excel instead — 10-100 shadow apps per mid-size company
3. **Maintenance debt**: The "Excel wizard" moves on, nobody understands the tool, business logic is opaque
4. **Can't scale**: Excel tools hit a wall — new features can't be added, implementation wasn't designed to scale
5. **Key-person dependency**: Critical business processes depend on one person's spreadsheet knowledge

## The SAXE Answer

SAXE reframes AI from "chatbot" to **"turn your domain expertise into real results."**

> "You know your business better than anyone. AI can now execute what you need — apps, research, offers, presentations — in a structured process you can trust."

### Narrative Flow

1. **Hook**: You're being asked to deliver AI impact. The tools available are either toys or generic.
2. **Twist**: The real opportunity isn't chatbots — it's turning your domain expertise into real results through structured AI processes.
3. **Mechanism**: Your processes are already defined — often in Excel, PowerPoint, or someone's head. SAXE takes that knowledge and runs it through an AI-guided pipeline: structured phases, reviewable artifacts, human approval at every gate. The first vertical is apps. The same model works for consulting deliverables, offers, due diligence, and more.
4. **Proof**: We've built 10 enterprise apps with this technology — for pharma, banking, biotech. The pipeline itself was built using this approach.
