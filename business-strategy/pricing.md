# Pricing Model

## Revenue Streams

### 1. Annual Platform License (recurring)

- Tiered by company size
- **Small company:** ~$20K/year
- **Large company:** ~$100K/year
- **Flat usage** — no per-pipeline, per-app, or per-user fees (beyond AI token costs)
- AI token costs are pass-through to the customer's own AI provider accounts
- Incentive: flat pricing encourages using more skill pipelines across verticals → increases stickiness and switching costs
- Platform license covers all skill templates (software, consulting, sales, custom) and open sessions

### 2. Deployment & Customization (one-time / project-based)

- Range: $10K–$200K depending on scope
- Includes: SSO/auth setup, corporate design system, architecture adjustments (e.g., .NET backend), skill template customization, deployment target configuration
- Can be delivered by PostXL team or certified partners

### 3. Scaling & Maintenance (ongoing professional services)

- When Saxe-built apps outgrow self-service and need professional maintenance
- Performance optimization, custom backend development, infrastructure scaling

## Unit Economics (rough)

- Average customer first year: ~$100K (license + deployment)
- Average recurring: ~$50K/year (license only)
- Partner model: TBD — potential revenue share or reseller discount

## Pricing Implications of Generalized Model

- Flat platform license naturally encourages adoption across verticals — a company that starts with software pipelines can add consulting or sales pipelines at zero marginal license cost
- Deployment & customization revenue grows with each vertical (new skill templates, artifact viewers, integrations)
- Partner opportunity expands: consulting firms can sell SAXE not just for app building, but for their own consulting delivery workflows
- Open sessions included in platform license — low marginal cost, high stickiness
